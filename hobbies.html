🏊 Swimming

I have been swimming competitively since I was nine years old. Swimming taught me discipline, time management, and the value of showing up even when it's hard. Early morning practices before school are tough — but there's nothing like the feeling of finishing a race you've trained hard for. I swim freestyle and backstroke, and I particularly enjoy relay events because of the teamwork involved.

Things I Love About Swimming

  • The meditative rhythm of laps — it clears my mind
  • Relay races — the team energy is unlike anything else
  • The discipline it builds that carries into everything else
  • Early morning practices (yes, I actually like them now)
  • Competing and pushing my personal best times

💃 Dance

Dance is my creative outlet. I have been trained in Bollywood — a Indian dance form — for over seven years, and I also enjoy contemporary and freestyle dance. Dance connects me to my culture and gives me a way to express things that words sometimes can't. I perform at school events and local cultural festivals. Every performance, no matter how small, teaches me something new about presence and expression.

✉️ Letters of Love (Club)

Letters of Love is a volunteer club where members write handwritten letters to people who are going through difficult times — patients in hospitals, seniors in care homes, and children in shelters. I joined in Grade 10 and became a team lead in Grade 11. It's taught me that small gestures carry real weight, and that empathy is a skill you practise, not just a feeling. This club connects directly to my interest in patient-centered care.
